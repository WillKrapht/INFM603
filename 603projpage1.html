Who We Are and What We Do

We are a new nonprofit organization working with the Prince George’s County government to provide supplies to those in quarantine. Due to the coronavirus pandemic, many individuals who are suffering from the virus are in strict quarantine and cannot travel to obtain groceries or home staples. We are aiming to help these individuals by providing some supplies and care. We have nine food banks that individuals donate food to, and our volunteers deliver it to our people quaranting due to COVID-19 at their convenience. We keep all dietary restrictions and needed items in mind, so that no one ends up with unusable goods and food goes to a good place. We’re happy to help anyone who needs it. Simply use the form below, let us know what you need, and we’ll be on our way.

Delivery Locations

We deliver to the homes of people within Prince George’s County who tested positive for COVID-19, or have been exposed to a COVID-19 patient and are consequently quarantined. Since they don’t have the means to get groceries and necessary supplies, we are providing them our delivery services to aid those in need.

Locations include:

  1. Bridgeway Community Cupboard
  2. Glenelg United Methodist Church
  3. Rachell L. Gray Community Foundation
  4. Ellicott City Head Start Center
  5. North Laurel Multi-Service Center
  6. Solomons Porch Worship Center
  7. FIRN
  8. Gethsemane United Methodist Church
  9. SAFE Food Pantry

How You Can Help

You can volunteer at any of the centers above by clicking the link "How to Help" tab above. Volunteers help pack food boxes and deliver these boxes to clients. Please note, we require our delivery volunteers to provide a confirmed negative COVID-19 test to prevent further spread of the virus. If you would like to donate you can also drop off food at any of the food bank locations.
